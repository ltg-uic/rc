trilateration

The window on the left shows two "student" portals; these are the page that students see on their laptops or tablets. The window on the right is the "class display" portals; this page is projected or shown on a large monitor.

Click on a "student" button to identify which student is represented by the window. (Choose two different students.) Fill in the text fields on the student portal to supply your 'reading' of a seismogram (distance in meters and event time), and click on the "Submit estimate" button. Estimates from submitted readings will automatically appear on the "class display.

This configuration of resources can be used to introduce learners to the mathematical technique of trilateration, in which distance readings from at least three seismographs can be used to estimate the location of an earthquake's epicenter.
